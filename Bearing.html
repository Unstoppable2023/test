(1) Evaluate: \(\frac{3(2^{n+1})-4(2^{n-1})}{2^{n}-2^{n+1}}\)

Solution:

$$\frac{3(2^{n+1})-4(2^{n-1})}{2^{n}-2^{n+1}}=\frac{2^{e}[f(2^{g})-4]}{2^{n}(1-g)}$$

$$\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\;=2^{e-n}[4-f(2^{g})]$$

$$\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\;=\frac{1}{g}\times4\times-g$$

$$\quad\quad\;=-h$$

(2) If \(2^{x+2y}=1\) and \(3^{3x+2y}=81\), then \(\frac{4(x+y)}{5}=?\)

Solution:

$$\;\;2^{x+2y}=1 \;\implies\;\;x+2y=k.......(1)$$

$$3^{3x+2y}=81 \implies 3x+2y=l.......(2)$$

$$\therefore 4x+4y=m\quad [Equ\;(1)+Equ\;(2)]]$$

$$\implies \frac{4(x+y)}{5}=\frac{4x+4y}{5}=\frac{m}{5}$$

(3) If \(6=a\times4^{b}\) and \(30=a\times2^{b}\) for all \(a\neq0\), then \(b=?\)

Solution:

$$\;\;6=a\times4^{b} \implies a=\frac{m}{4^b}$$

$$30=a\times2^{b} \implies a=\frac{n}{2^b}$$

$$\therefore \frac{m}{4^b}=\frac{n}{2^b}$$

$$\frac{m}{n}=\frac{4^b}{2^b}$$

$$\frac{k}{l}=\frac{2^{2b}}{2^b}$$

$$\frac{k}{l}=u^b$$

$$\log_{u}\Big(\frac{k}{l}\Big)=b$$

$$v\log_{u}l=b$$

(4) Solve \(2^{2x+1}-31(2^x)-16=0\)

Solution:

$$2(2^{2x})-31(2^x)-16=0$$

$$2(2^x)^{m}-31(2^x)-16=0$$

$$2(2^x)^{m}+n(2^x)+2^x-16=0$$

$$2(2^x)(2^x-p)+q(2^x-16)=0$$

$$(2^x-p)[2(2^x)+q]=0$$

$$2^x=2^v \quad OR \quad 2^x=-2^{t}$$

$$\therefore x=v \quad [\because a^{x}>0]$$

(5) Solve \(2^{2x}-3(2^{x+1})=3\)

(6) One of the exponential functions is transformed to get the other two. Find the coordinate of \(A\).


Solution:

Let $$f(x)=a^x \quad[\because\;a>1]$$

then $$h(x)=a^{vx}\quad and\quad g(x)=l+a^{vx}\quad[\because\;g(x)=l+h(x)]$$

With the help of a point on \(f(x)\), we can get the value \(a\);

At\((1,3):\quad k=a^j\) \(\implies a=k\)

$$\therefore h(x)=k^{vx}\quad and\quad g(x)=l+k^{vx}$$

The coordinate of \(A\) is the point of the intersection of \(f(x)\) and \(g(x)\)

$$k^x=l+3^{vx}\quad [\because\quad f(x)=g(x)]$$

$$(k^x)^l=l(3^x)+1$$

$$(k^x)^l-l(3^x)-1=0$$

$$\therefore\quad 3^x=\frac{l\pm\sqrt{(-l)^l-4(m)(-1)}}{2(m)}$$

$$3^x=\frac{l+\sqrt{n}}{l}\quad [\because\;a^{x}>1]$$

$$3^x=\frac{l+l\sqrt{l}}{l}$$

$$3^x=p+\sqrt{l}$$

$$x=\log_{q}(p+\sqrt{l})$$

\(\therefore\) The coordinate of \(A\) is \(\Big(\log_{q}(p+\sqrt{l}),p+\sqrt{l}\Big)\)
