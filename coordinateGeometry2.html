SECTION B

(1) If \(x^2+y^2+2x-8y+8=0\) is shifted \(4\) units to the right and then \(2\) units down. What is the resulting equation?

Solution:


$$x^2+y^2+2x-8y+8=0$$

$$(x-h)^2-m+(y-k)^2-n+8=0$$

$$(x-h)^2+(y-k)^2=r^2$$

\(\therefore\) The resulting equation is

$$(x-h-a)^2+(y-k+b)^2=r^2$$

$$(x-r)^2+(y-b)^2=r^2$$

(2) Find the equation of the line tangent to the circle at \(A(4,-1)\) as shown below.

Solution:


$$\angle OAB=z^{\circ}\quad[Tangent\;Line\;and\;Radius\;Relationship]$$

$$x^2+y^2-4x-2y-3=0$$

$$(x-h)^2-m+(y-k)^2-k-3=0$$

$$(x-h)^2+(y-k)^2=(h\sqrt{h})^2$$

The slope of \(OA\) is $$\frac{k+1}{h-4}$$

\(\therefore\) The equation of \(AB\) is

$$ y+1=\frac{4-h}{k+1}(x-4) $$

$$ ax-ay=b$$

(3) Find the length of tangent line \(|AB|\).

Solution:


$$\angle OAB=z^{\circ}\quad[Tangent\;Line\;and\;Radius\;Relationship]$$

$$x^2+y^2-8x+8y+19=0$$

$$(x-h)^2-m+(y-k)^2-m+19=0$$

$$(x-h)^2+(y-k)^2=(\sqrt{r})^2$$

\(\therefore |AB|=\sqrt{(h-12)^2+(k+5)^2-r}=u\sqrt{r}\quad[Pythagorean\;Theorem]\)

(4) Find the equation of the circle.

Solution:


$$\angle PQR=m^{\circ}\quad[Thale's\;Theorem]$$

Once we know the center of the circle and its radius, we can then get the equation. But we are not given the x-coordinate of \(Q\). Let the x-coordinate of \(Q\) be \(t\), then

$$\quad\quad\quad\frac{a-5}{a+1}=-\frac{a-t}{a-0}\quad[\because |PR|\perp|QR|]$$

\(\implies\)$$t=\frac{u}{v}$$

Let the coordinate of the center be \((h,k)\), then

$$(h,k)=\Big(\frac{-1+t}{b},\frac{5+0}{b}\Big)=\Big(\frac{c}{d},\frac{5}{b}\Big)$$

$$|OQ|^2=\Big(\frac{c}{d}-\frac{u}{v}\Big)^2+\Big(\frac{5}{b}-0\Big)^2=\frac{r}{s}\quad[Distance\;b/w\;two\;points]$$

\(\therefore\)The equation of the circle is $$\Big(x-\frac{c}{d}\Big)^2+\Big(y-\frac{5}{b}\Big)^2=\frac{r}{s}$$

(5) Find the center and the radius of the circle.

Solution:


The general equation of a circle is $$x^2+y^2+ax+by+c=0$$

At \(A(0,5):\)$$ub+c=-(u^2).......(1)$$

At \(A(0,-1):\)$$-vb+c=-v.......(2)$$

At \(A(6,5):\)$$ma+ub+c=n......(3)$$

\(\therefore\)$$b=\frac{p}{m}=q\quad[Equ(1)-Equ(2)]$$

$$c=vb-v=u\quad[From\;Equ(2)]$$

$$a=\frac{n+ub-c}{m}=-m\quad[From\;Equ(3)]$$

The equation of the circle is $$x^2+y^2+ax+by+c=0$$

$$(x-h)^2+(y-k)^2=(h\sqrt{k})^2$$

\(\therefore\)The center of the circle is \((h,k)\) and the radius is \(h\sqrt{k}\)
